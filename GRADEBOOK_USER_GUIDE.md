## For Teachers and Non-Technical Users

## рҹҺҜ What is the Gradebook?

The **Gradebook** is where you enter student scores for quizzes, projects, and exams. The system automatically calculates final grades using the official DepEd K-12 grading formula. No manual calculation needed!

---

## рҹҡҖ Getting Started (3 Easy Steps)

### Step 1: Select a Subject
Click the first dropdown and choose the **subject** you're teaching (e.g., Mathematics, English, Science).

### Step 2: Select a Section  
Click the second dropdown and choose your **class section** (e.g., Einstein, Newton, Grade 7-A).

### Step 3: Select a Quarter
Click the third dropdown and choose the **grading period** (e.g., 1st Quarter, 2nd Quarter).

> рҹ’Ў **Tip:** All three selections must be made before the gradebook appears!

---

## рҹ“қ How to Enter Grades

### Method 1: Click and Type
1. **Click** on any empty cell under a quiz or activity
2. **Type** the student's score (e.g., 8, 15.5, 42)
3. **Press Enter** or **click outside the box**
4. вң… A **green checkmark** appears = Grade saved!

### Method 2: Tab Through Cells
- Press **Tab** to move to the next student
- This is faster when entering many grades at once

### What if I make a mistake?
- Simply **click the cell again** and type the correct score
- The system automatically updates the grade

---

## рҹ“Ҡ Understanding the Columns

### рҹ”ө **WW** = Written Works
- Quizzes, seatwork, homework
- Usually worth **20-40%** of the final grade
- Blue badges in the gradebook

### рҹҹЈ **PT** = Performance Tasks  
- Projects, demonstrations, experiments, presentations
- Usually worth **40-60%** of the final grade
- Purple badges in the gradebook

### рҹҹ  **QA** = Quarterly Assessment
- Major exams at the end of the quarter
- Usually worth **20%** of the final grade
- Orange badges in the gradebook

> рҹ“Ң **Note:** The exact percentages depend on your subject settings

---

## рҹ§® How Grades are Calculated

### The Simple Explanation:
The system uses the **DepEd K-12 Weighted System**. Here's how it works:

1. **Add up all scores** in each category (WW, PT, QA)
2. **Calculate percentage** for each category
3. **Multiply by weight** (e.g., 30%, 50%, 20%)
4. **Sum them all** = Final Grade

### Example:
If a student earned **80 out of 100** on Written Works (worth 30%):
- 80 Г· 100 = **80%**
- 80% Г— 30% weight = **24 points** toward final grade

The system does this automatically - you just enter the scores!

## вқ“ Common Questions

### Q: Can I edit a grade after saving it?
**A:** Yes! Just click the cell again and type the new score.

### Q: What if a student was absent?
**A:** Leave the cell empty (or enter 0 if appropriate). The system still calculates based on available scores.

### Q: Why can't I see the gradebook?
**A:** Make sure you've selected all three: Subject, Section, and Quarter.

### Q: Can I print the gradebook?
**A:** Yes! Use your browser's print function (Ctrl+P or Cmd+P).

### Q: What if I see a warning about invalid score?
**A:** You entered a number higher than the maximum score. Check the "/XX" under the activity name.
